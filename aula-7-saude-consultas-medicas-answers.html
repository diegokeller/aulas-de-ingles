Exercise 5: Dictation Transcript

  1. If you are feeling very dizzy, the doctor said you should rest at home until you feel better.
  2. You must not drive tonight because the medicine might cause some drowsiness.
  3. You have to check at the front desk to see if your health insurance covers the consultation.

Exercise 6: Conversation (Situational Practice)

(Teachers should guide students to use specific terms in each scenario)

  1. At the Pharmacy: Focus on: "I have a headache/rash", "Do you have an OTC painkiller?", "Are there side effects?".
  2. Monday Morning: Focus on: "I'm under the weather/Monday morning blues", "I should stay home", "Do I have to bring a prescription?".
  3. At the Gym: Focus on: "You look as fit as a fiddle", "I feel dizzy", "I might need a check-up".
  4. Medical Emergency: Focus on: "He has an injury", "He is in the ER", "Does the health insurance cover this?".

Exercise 7: Reading Comprehension

  1. John made an appointment because he was feeling under the weather with several symptoms (headache, sore throat, swollen neck, dizzy) and noticed a large bruise.
  2. The GP said John must rest and should drink water and stay home from work.
  3. He doesn't have to take antibiotics because he has a viral infection (not bacterial).
  4. The pharmacist said he mustn't drive after taking the medication.
  5. The common side effect of the medicine is drowsiness.
