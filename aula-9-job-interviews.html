Disambiguation / Desambiguação

Resume vs. Summary

  • Resume: Documento profissional com experiência, formação e habilidades.
    Example: I updated my resume before applying for the job.
  • Summary: Resumo de um texto, apresentação ou ideia.
    Example: She gave a short summary of the project results.

Job vs. Work

  • Job: Cargo, emprego específico.
    Example: He got a new job in marketing.
  • Work: Trabalho em geral, atividade profissional.
    Example: I have a lot of work this week.

False Friends / Falsos Cognatos

  • Actually não significa atualmente. Significa na verdade ou de fato. Para atualmente, use currently ou nowadays.
    Example: I currently work in sales, but I am actually looking for a new challenge.
  • College não significa colégio. Significa faculdade.
    Example: She studied business in college.
  • Parents não significa parentes. Significa pais. Para parentes, use relatives.
    Example: My parents encouraged me to apply for the position.

Grammar Focus / Gramática em Contexto

Nesta aula, o foco gramatical é o Past Simple, muito usado em entrevistas para falar sobre experiências passadas, cargos anteriores, projetos concluídos e resultados alcançados.

Quando usar
  • Para descrever empregos anteriores: I worked at a startup for three years.
  • Para falar de ações concluídas: We launched a new product in 2024.
  • Para mencionar resultados ou responsabilidades específicas: I managed a small sales team.
Estrutura / Structure
Affirmative Negative Question
I worked in customer service. I did not work remotely. Did you work with international clients?
She led a project team. She did not lead the hiring process. Did she lead that department?
Verbos regulares e irregulares
Base Verb Past Simple Example

apply applied She applied for three positions.

Slangs & Idioms / Gírias e Expressões

To sell yourself

Significado: Apresentar bem suas qualidades e resultados, sem necessariamente soar arrogante.

Example: In an interview, you need to sell yourself with clear examples.

To be a good fit

Significado: Combinar bem com a vaga, equipe ou cultura da empresa.

Example: I believe I would be a good fit for this customer-facing role.

To wear many hats

Significado: Assumir várias funções ou responsabilidades diferentes.

Example: At my last startup, I wore many hats, from sales to operations.

To hit the ground running

Significado: Começar rápido e com eficiência desde o início.

Example: They wanted someone who could hit the ground running in the first month.

Abbreviations / Abreviações

  • HR: Human Resources.
  • CV: Curriculum Vitae.
  • CEO: Chief Executive Officer.
  • KPI: Key Performance Indicator.
  • FYI: For Your Information.

Exercise 6: Conversation Practice

Exercise 7: Reading Comprehension

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas em inglês.

When Marina saw a marketing position at an education company, she felt that the role matched her background perfectly. She had worked in content creation for four years, but in her last job she also helped with events, email campaigns, and customer communication, so she often wore many hats. The night before the interview, she updated her resume, reviewed her biggest achievements, and wrote down examples of difficult situations she had handled at work.

The next morning, the hiring manager welcomed her into a bright meeting room and asked her to talk about her professional journey. Marina explained that she started as a junior assistant, but later led a small creative team and helped launch a campaign that improved student engagement by 30 percent. When the interviewer asked about her strengths, she mentioned organization, clear communication, and the ability to stay calm under pressure. She also spoke honestly about one weakness: in the past, she sometimes tried to do everything alone. She said she learned to delegate better after managing a busy project with a short deadline.

Near the end of the conversation, Marina asked thoughtful questions about company culture, training opportunities, and the salary range. The interviewer seemed impressed, smiled, and said the company wanted someone who could grow with the team and hit the ground running. Two days later, Marina got a call from the recruiter inviting her to a final interview. For the first time in months, she left an interview feeling not just relieved, but genuinely excited about the next step in her career path.

1. Why did Marina believe the job was a good match for her?

2. What did Marina do the night before the interview?

3. What achievement did she mention during the interview?

4. What weakness did Marina mention, and what did she learn from it?

5. What happened after the interview, and how did Marina feel?
