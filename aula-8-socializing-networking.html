Disambiguation / Desambiguação

Meet vs. Know

  • To Meet: Usado para o primeiro encontro ou para encontrar alguém em um local marcado.
    Example: I met him yesterday at the party. / Let's meet at the mall.
  • To Know: Usado para quando você já conhece a pessoa (familiaridade).
    Example: I have known her for ten years.

False Friends / Falsos Cognatos

1. To Attend vs. To Assist

  • Attend: Significa "participar" ou "frequentar" (não "atender").
    Example: I attend meetings every Monday.
  • Assist: Significa "ajudar" ou "prestar assistência" (não "assistir" TV, por exemplo).
    Example: Can you assist me with these bags?

2. Pretend vs. Intend

  • Pretend: Significa "fingir" (não "pretender").
    Example: He pretended to be asleep.
  • Intend: Significa "pretender" ou "ter a intenção".
    Example: I intend to stay here for a week.

Grammar: Present Perfect (Experience)

O Present Perfect é um dos tempos mais importantes para o "Networking" porque ele conecta o passado com o presente. Usamos quando o momento exato não é importante, mas sim o fato de que algo aconteceu.

Quando usar? / When to use?
  • Experiências de Vida: Coisas que você já fez (ou nunca fez) na vida.
    Example: I have been to London three times.
  • Tempo não especificado: Ações que ocorreram em um momento qualquer do passado.
    Example: We have met before at a conference.
  • Conexão com o Presente: Algo que começou no passado e ainda é verdade ou relevante.
    Example: I have known Marco since high school.
Present Perfect vs. Past Simple

Essa é a maior dificuldade para brasileiros. Veja a diferença:

Present Perfect Past Simple
Tempo Indeterminado (Não dizemos quando).
I have seen that movie.		 Tempo Determinado (Dizemos quando aconteceu).
I saw that movie last night.
Ação que pode se repetir ou continua.
I have met many CEOs in my career.		 Ação finalizada em um ponto do tempo.
I met the CEO yesterday.
Estrutura / Structure

Have / Has + Past Participle (Particípio Passado)

Affirmative Negative Question
I/You/We/They have met him. I haven't met him yet. Have you ever met him?
He/She/It has attended. She hasn't attended. Has he ever attended?
Uso de Palavras-Chave / Using Key Words

Note a posição dessas palavras (geralmente entre o have/has e o verbo):

  • Ever (já / alguma vez): Usado apenas em perguntas sobre experiência.
    Example: Have you ever worked abroad?
  • Never (nunca): Usado para experiências negativas. O verbo fica na afirmativa.
    Example: I have never used this software.
  • Already (já): Usado para algo que aconteceu antes do esperado.
    Example: We have already exchanged contacts.
  • Yet (ainda / já): Usado em negativas e perguntas, geralmente no final da frase.
    Example: I haven't finished the report yet.

Slangs & Idioms / Gírias e Expressões

To break the ice

Significado: Iniciar uma conversa em um ambiente desconfortável ou desconhecido.

Example: He told a joke to break the ice at the networking meeting.

To touch base

Significado: Falar brevemente com alguém para se atualizar ou manter contato.

Example: Let's touch base next week to discuss the project.

To hit it off

Significado: Dar-se bem imediatamente com alguém.

Example: They met at the conference and really hit it off.

To work the room

Significado: Interagir com várias pessoas em um evento social ou de networking.

Example: A good salesperson knows how to work the room effectively.

To read the room

Significado: Perceber o clima ou as emoções das pessoas em um ambiente antes de falar ou agir.

Example: You should read the room before telling that joke; everyone looks very serious.

Abbreviations / Abreviações

  • RSVP: Répondez s'il vous plaît (Responda, por favor - usado em convites).
  • BYOB: Bring Your Own Beverage (Traga sua própria bebida).
  • ASAP: As Soon As Possible (O mais rápido possível).
  • TBA: To Be Announced (A ser anunciado).
  • ETA: Estimated Time of Arrival (Horário estimado de chegada).

Exercise 6: Conversation (Verbal)

Pratique as seguintes situações com seu professor ou colega:

  2. The Party Guest: Você está em uma festa de um amigo em comum. Inicie uma conversa fiada (small talk) com alguém que você não conhece.

Exercise 7: Reading Comprehension

The Annual Gala

Last night, I attended the annual industry gala at the Grand Hotel. I have never been to such a large event before! As soon as I arrived, I saw a mutual friend from my previous job. We hadn't seen each other in years, so we spent some time catching up on our careers and families. It was wonderful to see how much she has achieved lately. She introduced me to some of her new partners, which was a great networking opportunity.

After that, I decided to work the room. I met a very interesting designer named Marco. To break the ice, I asked him about his thoughts on the keynote speaker. We really hit it off and talked for almost thirty minutes. However, I had to read the room when the CEO started his speech; everyone became silent and attentive. Marco and I have already exchanged business cards, and I intend to touch base with him next week to discuss a potential partnership. I am glad I didn't decline the invitation.

Later in the evening, the host announced a surprise gathering for the top contributors. Although I was a bit tired, I decided to show up and mingle with the other guests. I met several people I had only known through LinkedIn before today. It was much better to meet them in person! Before I left, I received an invitation to a private seminar next month. The ETA for the follow-up email is tomorrow morning. I was happy to accept it immediately and I've already sent my RSVP!

Questions:

1. Had the narrator ever attended such a large event before? Which grammar tense is used to express this?

2. How did the narrator's mutual friend help them with networking?

3. Why did the narrator have to "read the room" while talking to Marco?

4. What is the difference between how the narrator "knew" the people at the gathering versus meeting them last night?

5. What has the narrator already done regarding the private seminar invitation?
