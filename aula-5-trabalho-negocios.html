Disambiguation

TermExplanation and Examples
Job vs. Work Job is the specific position/occupation (countable). Work is the activity or the place (uncountable).
Ex: I love my job. / I have a lot of work to do today.
Customer vs. Client Customer buys products (shops). Client buys professional services (lawyers, consultants).
Ex: The shop has many customers. / The law firm has a new client.

Slangs and Idioms

Slang / IdiomMeaning and Example
To talk shop To talk about work outside of office hours.
Example: We went to the bar, but we just talked shop all night.
Get the ball rolling To start a project or an activity.
Example: Let's get the ball rolling on this new marketing campaign.
Win-win situation A result that is good for everyone involved.
Example: This partnership is a win-win situation for both companies.
Deal breaker Something that causes an agreement to fail.
Example: The lack of a remote work policy was a deal breaker for me.

Exercise 7: Reading Comprehension

The New Project

Last Monday, my manager called a meeting to talk about a new project. Our company has a big client in the technology sector, and we need to deliver a complete report by the end of the month. The deadline is very tight, so we need to improve our workflow to be more efficient. I have a lot of work to do, but my colleagues are helping me. We discussed the budget and agreed that it is a win-win situation for everyone. My manager wants me to send the first draft ASAP so he can give me some feedback.

1. Who called the meeting last Monday?

2. What does the company need to deliver by the end of the month?

3. Why does the team need to improve their workflow?

4. What did the team discuss and agree on?

5. When does the manager want the first draft?
