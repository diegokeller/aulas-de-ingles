Lesson 6: Shopping and Sales

Goals

  • Aprender o vocabulary essencial para compras, descontos e métodos de pagamento.
  • Praticar o uso de comparatives e superlatives para comparar preços e qualidade de produtos.
  • Desenvolver habilidades de conversação para lidar com situações reais em lojas.

Disambiguation

TermExplanation and Examples
To Buy vs. To Purchase To buy is more common/informal for daily items. To purchase is more formal, often used in business or for expensive items.
Ex: I need to buy some milk. / The company purchased a new building.
Cash vs. Change Cash refers to physical money (bills/coins). Change is the money you get back when you pay with more than the price.
Ex: Do you have any cash? / Here is your change, sir.

Grammar: Comparatives and Superlatives

1. Comparatives (Comparativos)

Usamos o comparative para comparar duas coisas, pessoas ou lugares. A estrutura básica é:

[Item A]1 + [Verb]2 + [Comparative Adjective]3 + than4 + [Item B]5
  • Adjetivos Curtos (1 sílaba): Adicionamos -er.
    Example: [This shirt]1 [is]2 cheaper3 than4 [that one]5.
  • Terminados em -y: Trocamos o -y por -ier.
    Example: [Online shopping]1 [is]2 easier3 than4 [going to the mall]5.
  • Adjetivos Longos (2+ sílabas): Usamos more antes do adjetivo.
    Example: [This watch]1 [is]2 more expensive3 than4 [my old one]5.

2. Superlatives (Superlativos)

Usamos o superlative para destacar uma característica extrema dentro de um grupo. A estrutura básica sempre usa o artigo the:

[Subject]1 + [Verb]2 + the3 + [Superlative Adjective]4 + [Group/Place]5
  • Adjetivos Curtos (1 sílaba): Adicionamos -est.
    Example: [This]1 [is]2 the3 cheapest4 [store in the city]5.
  • Terminados em -y: Trocamos o -y por -iest.
    Example: [Friday]1 [is]2 the3 busiest4 [day for shopping]5.
  • Adjetivos Longos (2+ sílabas): Usamos the most antes do adjetivo.
    Example: [This]1 [is]2 the3 most beautiful4 [dress in the shop]5.

3. Irregular Adjectives

Alguns adjetivos não seguem as regras acima e mudam completamente:

AdjectiveComparativeSuperlative
Good (Bom)Better thanThe best
Bad (Mau)Worse thanThe worst
Far (Longe)Farther/Further thanThe farthest/furthest

Exercise 8: Reading Comprehension

The Big Summer Sale

Last Saturday, Julia went to the shopping mall because there was a big summer clearance sale. She visited three different stores to compare prices and styles. In the first store, she found a beautiful silk dress, but it was the most expensive one she saw. It cost an arm and a leg, so she decided to keep browsing. In the second store, the dresses were cheaper than in the first one, but the quality wasn't very good. Finally, in the third store, Julia found a steal: a designer dress that was 70% on sale! It was the best bargain of the day. She went to the checkout, paid in cash, and made sure to get her receipt. Julia was very happy because she couldn't afford the full price, but with the discount, she bought a high-quality brand for a low price. She is definitely not a shopaholic, but she loves a good deal!

1. Why did Julia go to the shopping mall last Saturday?

2. Why didn't Julia buy the dress in the first store?

3. How was the quality of the dresses in the second store compared to the first one?

4. What does the text mean when it says Julia found "a steal" in the third store?

5. What did Julia do at the checkout to ensure she could return the dress if necessary?
